# FounderContent AI Product Spec

## Product Summary

FounderContent AI is a founder-focused content product that helps startup builders turn experiences, lessons, and milestones into LinkedIn-ready content.

The long-term product vision is a **Founder Personal Branding OS**. The MVP is intentionally narrower:

**Idea -> Hook -> Post**

That workflow gives founders immediate value without pulling the product into scheduling, analytics, or broad marketing automation too early.

## Product Vision

FounderContent AI should become the system founders use to:

- discover what to post
- find stronger hooks
- turn founder stories into publishable content
- build a recognizable personal brand over time

In roadmap terms, the product grows toward a broader branding OS. In MVP terms, it remains a focused content workflow for LinkedIn.

## Positioning

FounderContent AI is not a general AI writer.

It is:

- a founder-specific content workflow
- a personal branding tool for startup founders
- a system for turning founder experience into content

Suggested messaging:

- Turn your founder journey into content.
- The personal branding OS for startup founders.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

- startup founders
- SaaS founders
- indie hackers
- founders building in public

### User Profile

These users usually:

- know they should post consistently
- want to grow audience, trust, and reputation
- prefer LinkedIn as their first content channel
- do not have a repeatable system for content creation

### Secondary Users Later

- consultants
- coaches
- local business operators

These audiences are intentionally deferred until the founder use case is proven.

## Problem Statement

Founders often know they should publish content, but they struggle with three specific problems:

1. They do not know what to post.
2. They do not know how to start a post strongly.
3. They do not know how to turn real founder experience into structured content.

Existing tools are either generic AI writers or broader marketing tools. They do not solve the founder-specific workflow well.

## Core Workflow

The MVP workflow is:

1. A founder generates content ideas.
2. The founder selects an idea and generates hooks.
3. The founder chooses a hook or angle and generates a full LinkedIn post.
4. The founder copies the final post and publishes manually.

This manual publishing step is deliberate. Human review stays in the loop, and the MVP avoids direct publishing or scheduling complexity.

## MVP Feature Modules

### 1. Founder Content Idea Generator

Route: `/founder-content-ideas`

Purpose:

- help founders decide what to post next
- generate founder-relevant topic angles

### 2. LinkedIn Hook Generator

Route: `/linkedin-hook-generator`

Purpose:

- generate strong post openings
- convert a topic into multiple framing options

### 3. LinkedIn Post Generator

Route: `/linkedin-post-generator`

Purpose:

- generate complete LinkedIn-ready posts
- provide multiple post angles for the same topic

## MVP User Experience Principles

The MVP should feel:

- fast
- simple
- specific to founders

Product rules:

- ask for only the minimum inputs needed
- show results in a copy-ready format
- let users move naturally from ideas to hooks to posts
- avoid dashboard-heavy UX in the first version

## Output Expectations

The core outputs should be:

- idea lists with clear founder angles
- hook sets with strong curiosity or storytelling openings
- three LinkedIn post variations for a selected topic

The product should optimize for usable output, not novelty for its own sake.

## SEO Entry Points

The first SEO pages should act as both traffic pages and product entry points:

- `/linkedin-post-ideas-for-founders`
- `/build-in-public-post-ideas`
- `/founder-storytelling-examples`

Each page should include:

- examples
- templates
- internal links to generator routes
- a clear CTA to use the product

## Scope Guardrails

The following are out of scope for the MVP:

- scheduling
- social account integrations
- analytics dashboards
- team collaboration
- multi-channel publishing
- workflow automation

These ideas belong to the future Founder Personal Branding OS roadmap, not the first release.

## Future Modules

The roadmap can expand into:

- LinkedIn post analyzer
- founder content library
- reusable post templates
- performance tracking
- recommendation and analytics layers

These are valid extensions, but they should not change the MVP definition.

## Success Criteria

The MVP is successful when a founder can:

- get a useful idea in under a minute
- generate several hooks quickly
- produce a LinkedIn-ready post without rewriting from scratch
- feel confident enough to copy and publish

The first product win is not feature breadth. It is clear value inside one repeatable workflow.
