# FounderContent AI SEO Strategy

## Objective

Use high-intent SEO pages to attract founders who are actively looking for content ideas, templates, and storytelling examples, then convert them into generator users.

The SEO strategy should support the same MVP workflow as the product:

**Idea -> Hook -> Post**

## SEO Thesis

The best early traffic is not broad "AI writing" traffic.

It is founder-specific search intent such as:

- needing LinkedIn post ideas
- looking for build-in-public examples
- searching for storytelling formats that work for founders

These users are already close to the product problem. The site should meet them with examples and immediately route them into the generator experience.

## Launch SEO Pages

### 1. LinkedIn Post Ideas for Founders

Route: `/linkedin-post-ideas-for-founders`

Purpose:

- capture high-intent founder content searches
- provide idea inspiration
- route readers into the content idea generator and post generator

Content focus:

- founder journey topics
- launch posts
- lessons learned
- customer insights
- product-building stories

### 2. Build in Public Post Ideas

Route: `/build-in-public-post-ideas`

Purpose:

- capture indie hacker and founder traffic
- serve milestone-driven and transparency-driven content searches
- route readers into hook and post generation

Content focus:

- milestone posts
- revenue or growth updates
- product progress updates
- mistakes and lessons learned

### 3. Founder Storytelling Examples

Route: `/founder-storytelling-examples`

Purpose:

- attract founders who want examples of strong narrative content
- showcase story formats that can be adapted into new posts
- route readers into the post generator

Content focus:

- failure stories
- pivot stories
- first-customer stories
- hard-lesson stories

## Page Structure

Each launch page should follow the same structure so the site is easy to scale.

### Section 1: Hero

Include:

- keyword-focused headline
- short explanation of who the page is for
- direct CTA to the relevant generator

### Section 2: Quick Examples

Include:

- fast-scanning lists
- 10 to 20 concrete examples near the top
- language that feels founder-specific, not generic

### Section 3: Templates

Include:

- reusable post patterns
- copyable starter structures
- clear framing for how founders can adapt them

### Section 4: Generator CTA

Include:

- a strong call to action to generate custom content
- links to the most relevant MVP route

### Section 5: Related Resources

Include:

- internal links to the other SEO pages
- internal links to generator routes

### Section 6: FAQ

Include:

- brief questions based on search intent
- explanations that reinforce founder relevance and create additional long-tail coverage

### `/linkedin-post-ideas-for-founders`

- `/founder-content-ideas`

- `/linkedin-post-generator`

### `/build-in-public-post-ideas`

- `/linkedin-hook-generator`

Secondary CTA:

- `/linkedin-post-generator`

### `/founder-storytelling-examples`

Primary CTA:

- `/linkedin-post-generator`

Secondary CTA:

- `/linkedin-hook-generator`

## Content Standards

Each page should:

- use founder-specific language
- provide real examples and not filler paragraphs
- keep content scannable with lists and short sections
- balance inspiration with direct product conversion
- stay aligned to LinkedIn-first use cases

Avoid:

- generic AI-writing content
- thin pages with only keywords
- off-topic startup advice unrelated to posting

## Internal Linking Strategy

Required internal link types:

- SEO page -> generator route
- SEO page -> related SEO page
- generator route -> SEO page

This supports both discovery and conversion.

Suggested link relationships:

- idea pages should link to hook and post pages
- storytelling pages should link to the post generator
- build-in-public pages should link to both hooks and full post generation

## Conversion Strategy

The SEO pages should not behave like standalone blog posts.

They should function as:

- search landing pages
- credibility pages
- conversion pages

Conversion mechanics:

- generator CTA above the fold
- in-content CTA after templates
- final CTA near the bottom

## Expansion Backlog

After the launch pages are live, future SEO expansion can include:

- `/startup-linkedin-post-ideas`
- `/startup-content-ideas`
- `/viral-linkedin-post-templates`
- `/saas-founder-content-ideas`
- `/linkedin-post-ideas-for-saas-founders`
- `/linkedin-post-ideas-for-indie-hackers`

These pages are valid future clusters, but they are not required for the MVP launch.

## Success Metrics

Track the following as the SEO engine matures:

- impressions by landing page
- click-through rate from search results
- CTA clicks into generators
- generator starts from SEO pages
- generator completions from SEO entry traffic

The real measure of SEO success is not traffic alone. It is qualified traffic that enters the Idea -> Hook -> Post workflow.
